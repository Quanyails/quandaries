# Step 4 – Fill Your Grid

<div>

Make sure your grid is fun to solve for, i.e.:

- A grid should not be unfinishable if they are stumped on a few select words, i.e. the grid should have multiple "paths" into certain areas of the grid.
- No two obscurities should intersect with each other.
- The player should not need specialized knowledge to solve one region of the grid (e.g. all sports clues or all movie clues).
- Don't use two words that can only be clued as the "same puzzle" (e.g. EAT + ATE). Publishers often extend this to entries that share the same root (e.g. EAT UP and ATE IN).
- You have more fun options for entries the longer they are, so try to strike a balance with how many "short" entries your grid has. E.g. publishers often ask that the # of 3-letter words in a crossword be kept within reasons.
